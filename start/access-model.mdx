Access to Soteris is tiered. Each tier sees a different slice of an engagement. The tiers describe who is reading, not how sensitive a given field is: a field stays sensitive regardless of who can reach it.

## Access tiers

| Access tier | Reader | What may be visible |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Public | Anyone, unauthenticated | Documentation, and verification of a released record's metadata via a receipt. |
| Partner | An integrated partner system or operator | Agreed lifecycle state and record shapes for shared engagements, within the mapping. |
| Soteris internal | Soteris operators and reviewers | Full engagement context, private working state, and review authority. |
| Agent-readable | An agent acting within a granted scope | Read models exposed for reasoning: lifecycle state, record shapes, and status, never internal judgment logic. |

## Access control is not content discipline

A tier decides who can reach a surface. It does not decide whether something belongs on that surface in the first place. Sensitive material, internal methodology, private reasoning, and client data must be kept off public and agent-readable surfaces at authoring time, regardless of who is currently permitted to read them.

Treat the [Disclosure boundary](/start/disclosure-boundary) as the rule that governs what may exist on a surface, and this page as the rule for who may reach it. Both must hold.
