Release readiness is an external-facing condition. It answers one question for a partner or agent: is there an authorized, available deliverable yet. It is not the internal approval process, and it does not expose the criteria that approval applies.

## What release means

When an engagement reaches release, it means:

1. A deliverable has been authorized by review authority.
2. A released record exists with its own version and access boundary.
3. A verification receipt can confirm the released record's metadata.

## What release does not mean

Release does not mean:

1. That you can see the reasoning behind the deliverable.
2. That the internal criteria for approval are disclosed.
3. That any private workpaper, evidence, or client data becomes visible.
4. That a public write surface now exists.

## Reading release from outside

From outside, treat release as a state plus a receipt. The state tells you a deliverable is available within your access. The receipt lets you confirm what was released. Everything upstream of release stays private. See the [Verification model](/verification/verification-model).
