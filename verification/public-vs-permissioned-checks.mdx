---
title: "Public vs permissioned checks"
tag: "Public"
---

A verification check discloses different amounts depending on who is asking. A public check confirms the minimum needed to trust that a release exists and is current. A permissioned check, available to a permitted party, can confirm more of the released record's metadata within that party's access.

## Disclosure by tier

| Check | Reader | Discloses |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Public | Anyone with a receipt | That a release exists, its version, and whether it is active or superseded. |
| Permissioned | A permitted party within scope | The above, plus visibility scope and release metadata agreed for that party. |

## What neither check discloses

<Info>
  Neither check exposes the reasoning, evidence, prompts, scoring, private workpapers, or client data behind a release. Permission widens which metadata is visible; it never crosses onto the issue side. See [What verification excludes](/verification/what-verification-excludes) and the [Access model](/start/access-model).
</Info>
