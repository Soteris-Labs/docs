Verification in Soteris is a verify-side model. It inspects the metadata of a released record and confirms its standing. It does not reveal, reconstruct, or explain the professional reasoning behind the deliverable. You can confirm that something was released and is current; you cannot derive how it was concluded.

## What verification answers

| Question | What verification can answer | What it cannot answer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Was this released by Soteris? | Yes or no, against a receipt. | Who concluded it, or how. |
| Which version is this? | The released version and its standing. | What changed in the reasoning. |
| Is it current or superseded? | The current status. | Why it was superseded. |
| Is it within my access? | The visibility scope. | The contents of private workpapers. |

## The boundary

<Info>
  Verification is intentionally thin. It exists to make releases confirmable, not to expose the work. Everything on the issue side, how the deliverable was prepared and judged, stays private. See [Public vs permissioned checks](/verification/public-vs-permissioned-checks) and [What verification excludes](/verification/what-verification-excludes).
</Info>
